IICE – World Bank – GITAIn 2019 – 2021, the Institute implemented a pilot technology transfer program in cooperation with the World Bank Group and the Georgian Innovation and Technology Agency (GITA). The project entitled: “New hydroelectrometallurgical technology for processing of manganese oxide raw material” envisaged indirect electrochemical recovery and leaching of manganese-containing recoverable ore in sulfuric acid solution to obtain ultra-high purity manganese sulfate solutions.The developed innovative hydroelectrometallurgical technology was used to process unreduced and reduced manganese-containing oxide ores supplied by the well-known South African company “Manganese Metal Company LLC” to obtain high-purity (≥99%) manganese sulfate monohydrate (HPMSM). Due to the mineralogical composition of the ores (high iron content), the processing of the South African unreduced oxide ore using the electroleaching method was carried out with a relatively low degree of manganese extraction (60-70%), in contrast to the Georgian ore, where the extraction degree was 98% and the processing of which achieved the receipt of high-purity (≥99%) HPMSM.2. IICE - Planetary Technology Inc.In 2022-2023, our institute collaborated with the Canadian company Planetary Technology Inc., on whose behalf it implemented a decarbonization-related project: “Indirect Electrogeneration of Magnesium Hydroxide in Water Splitting Electrochemical Cell”. Within the framework of the project, indirect electrochemical generation of the carbon dioxide capture reagent, magnesium hydroxide, from aqueous solutions containing magnesium sulfate was performed. The project was implemented on a scaled-up laboratory scale using an innovative approach and interesting findings. The results were reported at the conference: The 63rd Annual Conference of Metallurgists and published in the journal Proceedings of the 63rd Conference of Metallurgists, COM 2024, published by Springer Nature, p. 607–624.3. IICE - Ourea Lithium CorpIn October-December 2025, the Institute was involved in the implementation of the Ourea Lithium Corp (USA) project: “Electrochemical and Hydrometallurgical Recovery of Lithium from Lithium-Bearing Ores”. The work involved testing an innovative approach to recovering lithium from the so-called stubborn aluminosilicate ore, α-spodumene. The approach involved the use of electrochemically generated hydrogen peroxide as an ore-degrading promoter during acid leaching of the ore. During the course of the project, a number of difficulties were identified, the solution of which is planned to be worked out jointly with the customer.
